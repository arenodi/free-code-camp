Carlos Justiniano Ribeiro Chagas

Chagas's work holds a unique place in the history of medicine.

Time line of Dr. Carlos Chagas

  • 1879 - Born in Oliveira, Minas Gerais, Empire of Brazil
  • 1897 - Enroll in the School of Mining Engineering at Ouro Preto, but changed to the Medical School of Rio de Janeiro
  • 1902 - Graduates from Medical School of Rio de Janeiro
  • 1903 - Gets his M.D. with a thesis on the hematology of malaria
  • 1905 - Accepts a position in the port authority of Santos, SÃ£o Paulo, with the mission of fighting the malaria epidemic
  • 1906 - Returns to Rio de Janeiro and joins the Oswaldo Cruz Institute
  • 1909 - Discovers for the first time the Trypanosoma parasite in humans
  • 1912 - Is elected to the National Academy of Medicine and received the prestigious Schaudinn Prize for the best work in protozoology and tropical medicine
  • 1917 - Accepts Cruz's directorship of the institute
  • 1920 - Becomes the director of the Department of Health in Brazil
  • 1934 - Dies in Rio de Janeiro from a heart attack at 55 years of age
